Design System

Building a scalable Design System using React and Next.js

COMING SOON
YEAR 2025
CONTEXT

When I first started building my personal portfolio, it was written entirely in HTML, CSS, and vanila JavaScript. As the project grew, I realized that the codebase was becoming increasingly repetitive and difficult to scale. I found myself duplicating UI patterns, rewriting similar components, and manually maintaining styles across multiple pages. This lack of structure led to visual inconsistencies, slower development, and a growing maintenance burden. To solve this, I migrated the project to HTML, CSS, React, and Next.js, which allowed me to adopt a component‑based architecture. I decided to build a complete Design System, a foundation that would support the project’s growth and demonstrate my ability to think like a Product Designer.

Timeline

3 Months

ROLE

UX/UI Engineer

PROCESS

Research, Design tokens: typography, colors, spacing, grid and layout, components and tech implementation.

PROTOTYPE

PROBLEM

During the early iterations of the portfolio, I identified several issues commonly found in projects without a system:

These issues weren’t just aesthetic—they affected speed, clarity, and maintainability.

  • Repetitive UI patterns leading to visual inconsistencies.
  • Difficulties in scaling the codebase as the project grew.
  • Manual maintenance of styles across multiple pages.
  • Irregular spacing and alignment issues.
  • Typography without a clear hierarchy.
  • Lack of standardization between mobile and desktop views.
GOALS
  • Consistency: Create a unified visual language with clear guidelines.
  • Scalability: Enable new pages and components to be built without rework.
  • Efficiency: Reduce repetitive decisions and accelerate development.

Research

Before creating any components, I studied how established systems structure their foundations.

Key Findings

  • Material Design: Tokens and guidelines.
  • Shadcn/ui: Component library with a focus on accessibility and customization.
  • Tailwind: Spacing scale and token definitions.

Takeaways

Impact

Key Learnings

  • A Design System is about decision‑making, scale, and efficiency.
  • Tokens are the foundation of any system.
  • Even small projects benefit from systemic thinking.

Reflections

  • Building a design system requires a shift in mindset from creating individual components to thinking about the overall ecosystem.
  • Collaboration between designers and developers is crucial for the success of a design system.
