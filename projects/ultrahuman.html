Ultrahuman

Transforming Complex Health Data into Actionable Wellness Insights

COMING SOON

YEAR 2025
BRIEFING

The health market, specifically smart rings like Oura Ring, UltraHuman, and Whoop, represents a revolution in how we monitor our well-being. However, there is a significant opportunity to adapt and evolve this technology to better meet users' needs and realities.

Timeline

3 Months

ROLE

UX/UI Designer

PROCESS

Desk research, competitive analysis, interview, heuristic analysis, JTBD, HMW, Impact vs benefit matrix, MoSCoW method, hypothesis statement, journey map, moodboard wireframing, user testing, A/B testing.

TOOLS

Figma, Maze, Meet

PROBLEM

Data Interpretation: We believe users struggle to understand the practical meaning of the data collected by the ring because the metrics are presented in a technical and decontextualized manner, resulting in low continuous engagement with the app.

GOAL
  • Design the dashboard with a focus on the practical meaning of the data.
  • Implement a layered visualization system (from simple to detailed).
  • Create personalized narratives that connect data to real-life contexts.

Research

Competitive Analysis

The key findings suggest Ultrahuman can differentiate through metabolic health focus, positioning between premium and budget competitors, and creating unique value through continuous glucose insights

User Interviews

Conducted qualitative interview with 5 individuals who wears a ring (any, not only Ultrahuman) who are health conscius professional and fitness enthusiasts.

"Stress! The ring changed my life. I changed my work schedule because I know there is a Client that stresses me. So I always move him to the day before my day off." P1

"If there is a technical word I don't know, I google it." P4

"I feel physically better after wearing the ring. I did some routine changes based on it reports." 3P


Defining the solution

How might we translate complex health metrics into meaningful insights that support user retention and position the ring as a daily wellness companion?

Cost-Benefit Matrix

Prioritizing features based on user needs and business goals.

customer journey-mapping

The goal of this journey is to map the input and monitoring of data within the Ultrahuman app, identifying user actions, difficulties, and pain points, in order to then correlate these issues with the solutions proposed in the previous stages.

Complete user lifecycle (awareness → purchase → onboarding → daily use → long-term engagement)

User Persona

Jarvah Scrip, 32-year-old Marketing Manager | Health-conscious but time-constrained | Previous Fitbit user seeking deeper health insights Goals: Understand metabolic health, optimize energy levels, improve sleep quality without subscription fees Pain Points: Overwhelmed by complex health data, frustrated with cluttered interfaces, wants actionable insights Tech Comfort: Moderate - uses fitness apps but struggles with information overload.

Business Objectives

Intelligent health companion providing deep metabolic insights through continuous glucose monitoring integration, sleep optimization, and recovery tracking—all without ongoing subscription costs.
