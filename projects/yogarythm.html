Yogarythm

YEAR 2024

BACKGROUND

An article from Sarvodaya Hospital says that Yoga has become one of the fastest-growing wellness practices in the past dacade. Worldwilde, approximately 300 million people regulary practice Yoga. In the past 5 years alone, yoga practitioners in the US has increased by over 50%.

GOAL

Yoga is a great way to deal with stress relief in the form of a physical workout. I want to create an intuitive and interactive Yoga app that can help begginers to learn and also find online classes. Effectiveness will be measured by analyzing the number of videos streamed as well as number of users accessing the site.

ROLE

UX/UI Designer

Developer

PROCESS

User research, interviews, survey, competitive analysis, ideation, prototyping, testing, evaluation

TOOLS

Figma, Google jamboard, FigmaJam, VS code and GitHub

TECHNOLOGIES

HTML, CSS and JavaScript

PROBLEM

The number of people with anxiety and stress has increased during the pandemic making people seeking a better way to cope with it. How can we help begginers to find the right level yoga class?

Research

To understand the needs and goals of my target users, I conducted user research using a combination of methods such as interviews and competitive analysis.

User interview

I conducted online and in person interviews, to understand how the users feel, their needs and their motivations. I wanted to get more insights into their frustations, goals and what need to happen to improve the website.


I interviewed 5 individuals, age 21 - 50, who have a busy schedule life, begginers and aware of the benefits of yoga.

Empathy Map

Emily Hacker, 33, teacher, lives in California

The empathy map allowed me to identify the user pain points

  • User wants to be able to choose the teacher
  • Users are experiencing a lack of information on postures
  • User want a platform to learn from home
  • Users are experiencing a lack of guidance to choose their classes

Persona

Based on research findings, I created a persona who represents the target of the users. I wanted to identify what frustates and blocks the user from getting what they need from the app

Defining the problem statment

After gathering the findings from the research, I define the problem statement.

Problem Statment: Emily Hacker is a college teacher, who needs to learn yoga positions and find online classes for her level because it helps with stress reduction and develops a body awareness.

Flow Chart

Before starting the design I created a user flow for the most important task my persona will perform, it helps me to picture how users will move through the app.

User story: "As a college teacher I want to try many classes, so I will be able to find my favorite flow.""

Site Map

Based on the research and persona discovery, I brainstormed, compiled and organized the necessary website content and functionality. I decided to include the most vital information to make it clean and easy to navigate.

Starting the design

Lo-fi wireframe

Using the insights from the visual research and user interviews, I landed on a potential design that allowed users to easily find the core functions they needed, while allowing them to focus on individual tasks associated with completing an action.

UI Kit

For the UI, I focused on creating color palettes that represents the tranquility and harmony associated with yoga. Teal color, evoke feeling of calmness, balance, and connection to the nature, which align well with the principles of yoga. The light blue color can help the users feel grounded and centered, facilitating their yoga and meditation practice.

Based on the feedback from initial usability test and insights, I created hi-fi wireframes and a prototype.

User testing revealed a list of priority revisions that I implemented to the high-fidelity prototype that included:

  • User want more information about the teachers background
  • User want a free option membership
