Terra Recycle

YEAR 2024

OVERVIEW

What amount of waste did you generate today? Perhaps a plastic bottle, some fruit peels, or a yogurt container? Have you recycled it? Are you sure that the waste was disposed of in the correct bin?


An aritcle from rePurpose website says: "Only about 9% of plastic is recycled, while the remaining 91% is either landfilled, incinerated, or dumped into the environment. Since the 1950s, humans have produced over 8.3 billion metric tons of plastic, and almost every piece still exists in some form." And that is about plastic, one of the easiest materials to know how to recycle.

ROLE

UX/UI Designer

PROCESS

User research, interviews, survey, benchmarking, ideation, prototyping, testing, evaluation

TOOLS

Figma, Miro, google form

PROBLEM

Every day tons of garbage is generated and most of it does not make it to the right bin in order to be recycled.

GOAL

  • Increase the recycling rates
  • Create an app to solve a problem people face daily when they want to recycle

CSD Matrix

Before jumping straight into research or solutions we decide to do a CSD Matrix to discuss, prioritize, and plan activities, track research progress, and surface remaning doubts using specific prompts for each category. The goal of this activity is not to reach consensus but gather and organize information from diverse perspectives.

The app must include educational information about recycling and waste management practices. Assuming that users are interested in using the app regularly and following its recommendations. How might we ensure that users maintain continuous use of the app and do not give up after a short period?
Users need to be notified of garbage and recycling collection days. Assuming that information about collection points and collection days will be easily available and up to date. How might we ensure that information about collection points and collection times is always up to date and accurate?
The application should provide a map with collection points for different types of waste. Assuming that local authorities supported and collaborated with the app initiative. Will the user have any help when accessing the app, on how they can separate their trash?
The app should allow users to find nearby recycling centers. Assuming there will be forms of monetization or partnerships that will support the application financially. What security and privacy measures will be needed to protect user data?
The app should allow tracking of the amount of recycled waste and provide reports on this. Assuming the app will be compatible with most mobile devices and operating systems. What will be the real impact of the app on reducing waste and increasing recycling? How can we measure and demonstrate this impact?
The app should encourage users to recycle. Assuming that the user will be rewarded in some way for maintaining good practices. How do you know if recycling is being done correctly?
The app will provide a form of reward for users who dispose of their waste correctly. Assuming users can navigate the app easily. What is the best approach to educating users about recycling?
The app should clarify in which trash can the item should be thrown. Assuming there will be clear instructions on how to use the app. What will be the real impact of the app on reducing waste and increasing recycling? How can we measure and demonstrate this impact?
The app should provide support users and their issues. Assuming the user has issues and they are resolved clearly with the application support. How can we measure and demonstrate this impact?
